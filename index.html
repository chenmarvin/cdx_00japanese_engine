Japanese Engine Help

Quick Start

  1. Choose a unit from the dark Japanese Engine dashboard.
  2. Read the current unit map and objective patterns.
  3. Practice with the five learning loops.
  4. Use the verb matrix and pattern generator.
  5. Write 3 to 5 original sentences in the writing box.
  6. Click Log practice when you finish a practice session.

Reading Support

Japanese words and sentences show hiragana readings in parentheses, such as 学校（がっこう）.

Main Tools

  • Practice Engine: five loops for recall, transformation, substitution, and conversation.
  • Verb Engine: one verb through dictionary, polite, negative, past, te-form, and potential forms.
  • Pattern Generator: combine subject, place/object, and verb into original sentences.
  • Portfolio Checklist: mark larger learning goals complete.

Efficient Daily Practice

1

Choose one unit and focus on one sentence system at a time.

2

Read the unit map first so you know the pattern, particle, or verb behavior being trained.

3

Work through the five learning loops and say the examples out loud.

4

Use the Verb Engine until each form feels automatic.

5

Use the Pattern Generator, then write 3 to 5 original Japanese sentences in the floating writing pad.

6

Move or resize the writing pad so it stays beside the examples you are studying.

7

Click Log practice only after you have produced your own sentences.

The goal is not just to recognize Japanese. The goal is to build the ability to produce sentences from reusable patterns.
